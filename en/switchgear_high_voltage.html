HIGH VOLTAGE SWITCH DEVICES (RUVN)

High-voltage switchgears (KRU) are designed to receive and distribute electrical energy of three-phase alternating current with a frequency of 50 Hz and a voltage of up to 35 kV in networks with an isolated or grounded neutral through an arc reactor.

KRU are used as high voltage switchgears (RUVN) in new construction, expansion, reconstruction and technical re-equipment in:

  • Distribution and transformer substations of urban electrical networks;
  • Distribution and transformer substations of civil facilities and infrastructure;
  • Distribution substations of industrial enterprises;
  • Traction substations of urban electric transport and the subway;
  • Step-down substations 35 (110) / 6 (10) kV and 6 (10) / 0.4 kV distribution networks;
  • Complete transformer substations of a high degree of factory readiness.

The enterprise produces switchgear for rated voltage 6(10) kV, 20 kV and 35 kV.

Complete switchgears KRU-1-6(10) UZ. 1 series KRU-Kaskad

Prefabricated unilateral service chambers (KSO) are a type of switchgear. Depending on the type of embedded switching equipment, the enterprise produces KSO 2XX series and KSO 3XX series.

KSO cameras of the 2XX series are used to solve more complex problems of protecting consumers of electricity, so in cameras of the KSO-202, KSO-298, KSO-215 and KSO-216T series, in contrast to the KSO-366 and KSO-314 series, where autogas load breakers of the VNA type, vacuum switches and microprocessor relay protection units are installed (the exception is the cells of the KSO-217 series, in which a vacuum load breaker of the VNVR-10 type is used as a switching device). The use of vacuum circuit breakers with a long service life of switching resistance significantly increases the reliability of power supply. In KSO 2XX series based on microprocessor protection units, maximum current protection, current cutoff, overload protection.

Prefabricated unilateral service chambers of the 2XX-th and 3XX-th series
